## UNIVERSAL BDS SELLING POINTS (apply to all products)

- **Dye-sublimation printing**: heat transfers dye directly into fabric fibers — colors don't fade, crack, or peel. Sharper and more vivid than vinyl or paper.
- **100% polyester stretch fabric**: wrinkle-resistant, machine washable, fire-retardant (B2 grade certified), eco-friendly
- **Tool-free assembly**: snap-lock aluminum tube frames — most products set up in under 10 minutes with no tools
- **Lightweight aluminum frames**: strong yet travel-friendly, break down into compact pieces
- **Free 2D mockups**: included on most products
- **5–7 business day production** + fast shipping globally
- **Machine-washable graphics**: reuse across multiple events; just wash and reuse
- **Eco-friendly**: dye-sub uses water-based inks, far less waste than vinyl/paper single-use prints
- **Interchangeable graphics**: swap fabric for new campaigns without buying a new frame
- **Custom sizing available** on most products

### The 3-Second Rule (use in design consultations)
If an attendee can't understand your message in 3 seconds, the design needs reworking. Advise: bold headline up top, strong visual center, clean call-to-action at bottom. Less is more.

### Tension Fabric vs. Vinyl (use when clients ask about materials)
Tension fabric: wrinkle-free, seamless look, folds neatly, doesn't crease, lightweight, eco-friendly, sets up in under 10 min, dye-sub colors are vivid and long-lasting.
Vinyl: durable, glossy finish, easy to wipe clean (good for food/drinks environments), heavier.
Muslin: classic, textured portrait look but wrinkle-prone.
**Recommendation for most trade show use**: tension fabric.

## TRADE SHOW STRATEGY INTEL (use in consultations)

### Booth Design Fundamentals
- **10x10 booths**: 100 sq ft of potential. Keep perimeter for banners/shelves/screens; keep center open and inviting. Use vertical signage to maximize floor space. Open concept invites in; enclosed can feel exclusionary.
- **10x20 booths**: Zone it — front for engagement/demos, midsection for product displays, rear for private meetings. Open/semi-enclosed concept options.
- **Lighting**: spotlights, LED strips, and backlit displays add depth and make graphics pop. Often underestimated. Backlit displays visible from further away.
- **Interactive elements**: touchscreens, product demos, AR/VR stations keep people in the booth longer. Live Q&A sessions create energy and draw crowds.
- **Lead capture**: QR codes, tablets, badge scanners > paper sign-in sheets. Train staff to qualify leads on the spot.
- **Follow-up**: the window of interest is short — send personalized email/call within days. Most clients wait too long.
- **Giveaways**: phone chargers, notebooks, tote bags > cheap pens. Brand on things people actually use.

### Selling Against Digital Displays
When a client says "we're thinking about a digital screen" — tension fabric counter-points:
- No screen fatigue: after hours of looking at tablets/phones, attendees crave physical branding
- No technical failure risk (no loading times, no glitches)
- Physical branding signals: "This business is prepared, professional, and invested"
- Far lighter and more portable than digital displays
- Fraction of the cost, but still a strong brand statement

### Banner Stand Placement Tips
- Near entry points and high-traffic areas for max visibility
- At eye level so messaging is immediately readable
- Pair with table covers, backdrops, and digital screens for cohesive look
- Add spotlights or LED accents to make display pop even in crowded halls
- Suspended/hanging banners visible from across the room = great for large venues

### Design Tips to Share with Clients
- **3-second rule**: if they can't get your message in 3 seconds, redesign
- Bold headline first; supporting visuals second; clear CTA third
- High contrast between text and background
- Brand colors + fonts = consistency and credibility
- Think of banner as a conversation starter, not a brochure — keep it simple
- "Instagrammable" setups get shared organically = free exposure
- Color psychology: warm tones (red, orange) evoke excitement; cool blues/greens convey calm/trust

## DIGITAL FABRIC PRINTING — TECHNOLOGY PITCH

(Use when explaining why BDS quality is superior)

- **Dye-sublimation**: heat transfers dye directly into fabric fibers; result is vivid, long-lasting colors that don't fade or crack; ink becomes part of the fabric itself
- **UV printing**: UV light cures ink instantly; extremely durable; sharp even under harsh trade show lighting
- **AI-assisted production**: automated processes reduce human error; real-time design adjustments ensure every print is flawless
- **Future trends**: AR-integrated fabrics (come alive via smartphone), LED-embedded fabrics, automated print customization (real-time graphic updates), projection mapping on fabric displays
- **Eco credentials**: water-based inks, recyclable materials, reusable displays = less waste vs. single-use vinyl; aligns with sustainability goals of modern brands
