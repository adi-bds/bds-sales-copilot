## Replacement Fabrics (Repeat Clients)

One of the most common repeat-client requests. Key points:

- **Can't order replacement fabric on the website** — rep must raise a custom invoice manually
- Always confirm the original frame was purchased from BDS before quoting
- If unsure, look up by client email in the system — check multiple email addresses if they're not the original orderer
- For SEG popup displays: quote front + back as separate fabrics (6 displays = 12 fabrics)
- Client can confirm shelf placements and other details once proof images are sent

**How to process:**
1. Confirm frame is BDS-purchased
2. Get dimensions and quantity
3. Raise invoice manually and share purchase link via chat/email
4. Send design template PDF to client if they don't have artwork ready
5. Client sends artwork to sales@backdropsource.com

## Product Comparison — What Clients Ask

### EZ Banner Stand vs. Portable Exhibit Display (Media Wall)
- Both: tubular frames, same fabric material
- **EZ Banner:** single wide-base foot
- **Portable Exhibit Display (Media Wall):** two feet — more stable, slightly wider stance
- Use EZ Banner for budget/lightweight setups; use Portable Exhibit for more premium/stable look

### SEG Foldable vs. SEG Modular
- **Foldable SEG:** panels connect with latch-like metal clamps on the back — folds for transport
- **Modular SEG:** no endcaps/clamps, true modular system (e.g., Model 9) — preferred for premium installs
- SEG popup: can be designed on **all four sides**
- SEG lightbox (modular): has aluminum profile on the sides — only front/back fabric

### SEG Lightbox — LED Lighting (Common Client Confusion)
Clients often ask why a wide display doesn't need lights in the middle. Jennifer's explanation:
> "The LED modules are embedded on the left and right sides through the full height of the frame. The illumination from both sides intersects at the middle, ensuring a bright, even backlit solution — no hotspots. Adding lights from all sides would actually cause interference and hotspots."

This is a closed hollow structure — fabric encloses front and back, keeping light contained within the system.

- LEDs run along **height** of the frame (left + right sides)
- Power supply is **external**, cable exits from **left side bottom**
- **Max height:** 10ft
- Frame profile: **80mm** up to 6.5ft H, **120mm** from 6.5ft H upward
- Default frame color: **metallic aluminum**
- Custom frame color (black or white): **+10 extra days, no significant price change**
- Footprint of a 16.4ft W display: occupies 16.4 x 8.2 floor space

### "Firefly" Lightboxes
Firefly is just a brand name for the same type of SEG lightbox product. BDS produces their own version. Not related to modular SEG kits.

## Multi-Address / Drop Shipping Orders

Clients sometimes need the same product shipped to multiple addresses (e.g., 6 office locations):
- Place the order with **one address** via the invoice link
- Client sends remaining delivery addresses via email to sales@backdropsource.com
- BDS will fulfill to all addresses from a single order — no need for multiple purchase links
- If one address needs a different spec (e.g., fabric + frame vs. fabric only), note it in the email

## Delivery & Timeline Management

- Always ask for the client's event/deadline date upfront
- Work backwards: give client buffer time (e.g., deliver by the 22nd if tradeshow is 26th)
- Proof image approval deadline matters — if proof isn't approved by a certain date, timeline can't be met
- Black/white custom frame color: add 10 extra days to standard turnaround
- If timeline is tight and custom color can't be met: suggest standard metallic aluminum and proceed

## Handling Complaints / Issues (Ryan Schwarz case)

Client received SEG popup displays but:
- Shelves were too high (proof approval was client's responsibility, but Jennifer didn't use that against them)
- Display cases had no front acrylic cover — client couldn't use them at consumer events
- Client had planned to order 40 units but was now hesitant

**Jennifer's approach:**
- Acknowledged the issue immediately ("No worries! We can fix this")
- Offered acrylic covers to send with replacement order
- Invited client to email her directly for future issues
- Stayed focused on resolution, not blame
- Result: Client stayed engaged and placed a new 34-unit banner order in the same conversation

**Lesson:** Even when a complaint arises mid-chat, resolving it warmly can convert it into a new sale. Don't get defensive.

## What to Confirm Before Quoting

Always collect these before giving a price:
1. **Product type** (which specific product?)
2. **Dimensions** (W x H, confirm units — ft or cm/mm?)
3. **Quantity**
4. **Single or double sided printing?**
5. **Timeline / event date**
6. **Delivery address / geo** (for shipping cost and warehouse routing)
7. **Any add-ons?** (TV mount, shelves, counter, lights, acrylic covers)
8. For replacement fabrics: **confirm original frame was from BDS**
