# BDS Sales & Order Workflow (Rep Training Guide)

**Source:** WorkFlow BDS.pdf — official internal training document

---

## Overview

Every order at Backdropsource follows an 11-step journey from first contact to delivery. The Sales Team owns steps 1–3 and 7. The Design Team owns steps 4–6. Production/Shipping owns steps 8–11.

Tools used: **Streak CRM** (email tracking & follow-up), **Shopify** (orders & payments), **Trello** (design team internal tracking only — not for customer comms).

---

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1 — Inquiry / Entry
Customers reach out via: Email, WhatsApp, website live chat, contact forms, website visitors, or abandoned checkouts.

- **Priority rule:** Direct customer inquiries are handled first.
- Abandoned checkouts and website visitors are followed up proactively to answer questions and help them move forward.

### Step 2 — Requirement Understanding
Sales rep collects the key details:
- Product type
- Size / dimensions
- Quantity
- Customization requirements (artwork, print, finish)

### Step 3 — Quote / Draft Order
- If the customer asks for a quote, or is clearly intent on ordering, create a **draft order in Shopify** with their details.
- Payment options available:
  - **Online** — via Shopify checkout link
  - **PO (Purchase Order)** — customer sends a PO
  - **Remittance** — bank transfer / wire
- For PO or remittance: customer must share **payment proof** before the order is confirmed.
- Once payment is made, the draft order becomes a confirmed order.

### Step 4 — Artwork Collection
- Ask the customer to share their artwork in **high resolution**.
- **Minimum spec: 150 DPI at full size.**
- Artwork sharing methods: WeTransfer, Dropbox, Google Drive, Email, or the website's Upload Artwork option.
- For large files, recommend cloud sharing (WeTransfer/Dropbox/Drive).
- If the customer doesn't have print-ready artwork, provide them with a **print template**.

### Step 5 — Design Stage
- Artwork is sent to the **Design Team**.
- Design Team creates a **2D mockup**.
- Trello is used internally by Design to track which orders need action — **not used for customer communication**.
- Proof/mockup is sent to the customer **via email**.

### Step 6 — Review & Revisions Loop
- Customer reviews the mockup and either:
  - **Approves** it, or
  - **Requests revisions**
- Revisions are made and re-sent until approval is received.
- **No fixed limit on the number of revisions.**
- Loop repeats until the customer gives final approval.

### Step 7 — Payment
- Customers can pay **before** seeing the mockup, or **after** reviewing it.
- **Mockups are never charged** — they are free regardless of how many revisions.
- Payment methods: Shopify (online), PO, Remittance.
- PO/remittance requires payment proof.

### Step 8 — Final Check (Gate to Production)
An order only moves to production when **both** of the following are true:
1. ✅ Payment is completed
2. ✅ Mockup is approved

Both must be confirmed before production begins. No exceptions.

### Step 9 — Production
- Order is moved into production.
- Timeline starts from the date both payment and mockup approval are confirmed.

### Step 10 — Shipping
- After production, the order is shipped to the customer's address.
- **International shipping:** customers must provide an **EORI number** (required for cross-border customs clearance).

### Step 11 — Delivery
- **Standard:** 5–7 business days from payment + approval date
- **Express:** 4–5 business days (additional cost)
- Note: delivery timelines may vary for certain products and heavily customised items.

## Quick Reference — Key Rules for Reps

| Situation | What to do |
|-----------|------------|
| Customer emails in | Log in Streak CRM, follow up from there |
| Abandoned checkout | Proactively reach out to assist |
| Customer wants a quote | Create a draft order in Shopify |
| Customer pays via PO or remittance | Get payment proof before confirming |
| Artwork needed | Request minimum 150 DPI; offer print template if needed |
| Customer wants changes to mockup | No limit on revisions — keep going until approval |
| "Is there a charge for the mockup?" | No, mockups are always free |
| When does production start? | Only after BOTH payment AND mockup approval |
| International customer | Remind them they need an EORI number for shipping |
| Standard lead time | 5–7 business days from payment + approval |
| Express lead time | 4–5 business days (extra cost) |

## Payment Methods Detail

- **Shopify Online** — send the customer a checkout link; payment is instant and auto-confirms the order.
- **PO (Purchase Order)** — customer sends a formal PO document; rep must receive and verify before confirming.
- **Remittance** — bank transfer; customer must send remittance advice / proof of transfer.

## Artwork Spec Quick Reference

- **Minimum resolution:** 150 DPI at full/final print size
- **Preferred method for large files:** Cloud (WeTransfer, Dropbox, Google Drive)
- **Template:** Offer a print template if customer doesn't have print-ready artwork
- **What happens next:** Design Team receives artwork → creates 2D mockup → sends proof via email

## EORI Number (International Orders)

EORI = Economic Operators Registration and Identification number. Required by customs authorities for shipments crossing international borders. If a customer outside the domestic market hasn't provided one, ask for it before the order ships. Without it, the shipment can be held at customs.
